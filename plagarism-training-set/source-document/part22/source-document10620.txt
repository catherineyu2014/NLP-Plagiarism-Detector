  It also gave rise to much license in issuing orders: too many people were giving
important directions, affecting the whole army, without authority from its head.  In view,
therefore, of all the errors that were committed from the time Chattanooga fell into our hands
after our first crossing the Tennessee, it was fortunate that the Union defeat was not more
complete, that it left in the enemy's possession not much more than the barren results arising
from the simple holding of the ground on which the engagement was fought.
